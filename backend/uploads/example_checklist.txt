### Compliance Checklist for IT Tool Procurement in a Health Company (EU)

#### 1. **Data Protection and Privacy**
   - Ensure compliance with General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).
     - Data collection, storage, processing, and sharing policies.
     - Data subject rights (access, correction, deletion).
     - Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) for new tools.
     - Appointment of a Data Protection Officer (DPO) if required.

#### 2. **Information Security**
   - Verify compliance with ISO/IEC 27001 standards.
     - Risk assessment and management protocols.
     - Incident response and recovery procedures.
     - Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments.

#### 3. **Health Data Regulations**
   - Adherence to Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) if applicable.
     - Safeguards for protected health information (PHI).
     - Secure electronic transmission and storage of health data.

#### 4. **Software and Vendor Assessment**

     - Background checks and certifications (e.g., ISO certifications).
     - Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and compliance guarantees.
   - Software compliance with medical device regulations (if applicable).
     - CE marking for medical software.

#### 5. **Interoperability and Standards**
   - Compliance with Health Level Seven International (HL7) standards.
     - Integration with existing systems (Electronic Health Records, EHR).
     - Use of standardized data formats and protocols.

#### 6. **User Access and Authentication**
   - Strong user authentication mechanisms.
     - Role-based access control (RBAC).
     - Multi-factor authentication (MFA).

#### 7. **Data Retention and Disposal**
   - Policies for data retention, archiving, and disposal.
     - Compliance with national and EU-specific retention requirements.
     - Secure deletion and destruction of data.

#### 8. **Audit and Monitoring**
   - Regular audits for compliance verification.
     - Monitoring and logging of access and activities.
     - Procedures for handling audit findings and non-compliance issues.

#### 9. **Transparency and Accountability**
   - Clear documentation of policies, procedures, and compliance measures.
     - Accountability mechanisms for compliance breaches.
     - Regular training for staff on compliance and security policies.

#### 10. **Ethical Considerations**
   - Ethical guidelines for data use and AI implementations.
     - Patient consent and transparency in data use.
     - Avoidance of bias and discrimination in tool usage.

#### 11. **Environmental and Sustainability Compliance**
   - Adherence to environmental regulations and sustainability practices.
     - Energy-efficient and environmentally friendly IT solutions.
     - Waste management and recycling policies.

#### 12. **Contractual Obligations**
   - Detailed contracts with vendors including compliance clauses.
     - Liability and indemnity provisions.
     - Terms for termination and data return/transfer.

#### 13. **Regulatory Compliance**
   - Compliance with relevant EU directives and national laws.
     - Regular updates and adaptations to comply with new regulations.
     - Consultation with legal experts for compliance verification.

By following this checklist, a health company in the EU can ensure that the procurement of IT tools aligns with all necessary legal, ethical, and security standards.
